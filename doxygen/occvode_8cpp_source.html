50 /*
51 CVODE expects dy/dt = f(y) and solve (I - gamma*J)*x = b with approx to
52 J=df/dy.
53 
54 The NEURON fixed step method sets up C*dv/dt = F(v)
55 by first calculating F(v) and storing it on the right hand side of
56 the matrix equation ( see src/nrnoc/treeset.cpp nrn_rhs() ).
57 It then sets up the left hand side of the matrix equation using
58 nrn_set_cj(1./dt); setup1_tree_matrix(); setup2_tree_matrix();
59 to form
60 (C/dt - J(F))*dv = F(v)
61 After a nrn_solve() the answer, dv, is stored in the right hand side
62 vector.
63 
64 However, one must be aware of the fact that the cvode state vector
65 y is not the vector y for the fixed step in two ways. 1) the cvode
66 state vector includes ALL states, including channel states.
67 2) the cvode state vector does NOT include the zero area nodes
68 (since the capacitance for those nodes are 0). Furthermore, cvode
69 cannot work with the extracellular mechanism (both because extracellular
70 capacitance is often 0 and because more than one dv/dt is involved
71 in some of the current balance equations) or LinearMechanism (same reasons).
72 In that case the current balance equations are of the differential
73 algebraic form c*dv/dt = f(v) where c is non-diagonal and may have empty rows.
74 The variable step method for these cases is handled by daspk.
75 
76 */
